// What changes

Three things every developer needs to understand

🪪

No more anonymity

Every APK distributed outside Play Store must be tied to a verified real identity — name, address, phone, ID documents. Pseudonymous publishing is dead.

🚧

High-friction installs

Even if you're registered, users installing your app face deliberate "Install without verifying" warning screens. Google is engineering distrust.

💀

F-Droid & alternatives at risk

Projects like F-Droid that host apps from anonymous contributors may not survive this. The open-source mobile ecosystem has no fallback plan yet.

Android's openness was never a bug. It was the point.
It's why developers built things that mattered.
It's why people in hostile environments could distribute tools safely.
It's why I publish my apps without asking permission from a corporation.

When Google kills anonymous sideloading, they don't just change a policy — they change who gets to build.
